Description

Gilman awards are for undergraduate study abroad and made to students who are receiving Federal Pell Grant funding at two-year or four-year colleges or universities. By supporting students who might otherwise not participate due to financial constraints, Gilman awards were created to diversify the types of students who apply to study abroad and the countries and regions where they go.

Eligibility

For detailed eligibility requirements, please see the Gilman webpage. In general, students should:

  • Be U.S. citizens.
  • Be an undergraduate in a full time post-secondary school program.
  • Be Pell Grant eligible (through Financial Aid).

Preparation

To prepare for submitting an application, students should:

  1. Carefully review the goals, eligibility requirements, and selction criteria for the Gilman scholarship.
  2. Review the list of questions frequently asked by applicants to learn about issues that applicants commonly face when applying for a Gilman award.
  3. Attend a Udall workshop to receive introductory guidance from our program on applying for a Gilman scholarship.
  4. You will be required to provide two essays on the Gilman scholarship application, each of up to 7,000 characters in length: a Statement of Purpose Essay and a Follow-on Service Project Proposal. Begin preparing for these essays by reviewing the essay guidelines provided online by the Gilman Scholarship Program.
  5. Schedule a meeting with the Director of the Distinguished Scholarships program. Come to your meeting prepared to take notes on how to best approach your application.
