Description

Marshall Scholarships provide funding for Americans to study for a graduate degree in the United Kingdom. As future leaders equipped with a lasting understanding of British society, Marshall Scholars strengthen the enduring relationship between the British and American people, their governments, and their institutions. Marshall Scholars are talented, independent, and wide-ranging, and their time as Scholars enhances thier intellectual and personal growth. Their direct engagement with Britain through its best academic programs at 168 higher education institutions in the U.K. contributes to their ultimate personal success. Up to 40 Marshall Scholarships can be awarded annually. They cover university feels, cost-of-living expenses, an annual book grant, a thesis grant, research and daily travel grants, fares to and from the U.S., and, where applicable, a contribution toward the support of a dependent spouse.

Eligibility

For a complete list of eligibility requirements, please consult the Marshall Scholarship webpage. In general, students should:
  • Be U.S. citizens.
  • Be a senior undergraduate or have graduated within two years from a first baccalaureate degree at the time of application.

Preparation

At Washington State University, students typically:
  1. Carefully determine their eligibility for this scholarship. Read carefully and thoroughly through the Marshall Scholarship website.
    1. Review the FAQs on the website to answer your specific questions.
    2. Determine how you will demonstrate your interest in attending a school in UK, and
    3. How your interests forward UK/US relations.
    4. Print a copy of the application questions (available online).
  2. Meet with the director to discuss ideas (appointments at DistinguishedScholarships@wsu.edu).
    1. After your meeting, draft your answers to the questions asked in the application on your word processor.
    2. Forward your draft to the director by email for review. You should plan to do several drafts of your work before the WSU deadline.
  3. Contact four to six recommenders to ask for a reference. Ask for a hard copy of these references one week before the WSU deadline (Friday, March 27, 2015) to be provided to Sarah Ann. (Tips for writing references)

    2. Chosen nominees will complete online applications after the review.
