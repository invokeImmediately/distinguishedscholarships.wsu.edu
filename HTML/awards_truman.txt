<h3>Description</h3>
<a href="http://www.truman.gov" target="_blank">Truman awards</a> recognize college juniors with exceptional leadership potential who are committed to careers in government, the nonprofit or advocacy sectors, education, or elsewhere in public service.

Truman awards provide up to $30,000 financial support for graduate student and leadership training. Scholars are required to work in public service for 3 of the 7 years following completion of their graduate degree.

<h2 id="eligibility">Eligibility</h2>
For a complete list of eligibility requirements, please consult the <a href="http://www.truman.gov/" target="_blank">Truman webpage</a>. In general, students should:
<ul>
	<li>Be U.S. citizens.</li>
	<li>Be a full-time junior undergraduate at the time of application.</li>
	<li>Complete the Truman <a href="http://www.truman.gov/are-you-potential-truman-scholar/" target="_blank">applicant checklist</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="preparation">Preparation</h2>
At Washington State University, students typically:
<ol>
	<li>Carefully determine their eligibility for this scholarship.</li>
	<li>Read carefully and thoroughly through the <a href="http://www.truman.gov/" target="_blank">Truman website</a>.
<ol>
	<li>Review the <a href="http://www.truman.gov/candidates/faq-candidates" target="_blank">FAQs on the website</a> to answer your specific questions.</li>
	<li>Determine how you will demonstrate your interest in being a change agent.</li>
	<li>Print a copy of the application questions (<a href="http://www.truman.gov/sample-application-materials/" target="_blank">available online</a>).</li>
	<li>Read Good and Bad responses to these questions (also <a href="http://www.truman.gov/effective-and-ineffective-responses/" target="_blank">available online</a>).</li>
</ol>
</li>
	<li>Meet with the Director of Distinguished Scholarships to discuss ideas (appointments at <a href="mailto:DistinguishedScholarships@wsu.edu">DistinguishedScholarships@wsu.edu</a>).
<ol>
	<li>After your meeting, draft your answers to the questions asked in the application on your word processor.</li>
	<li>Forward your draft to the director by email for review.</li>
</ol>
</li>
	<li>Contact two-three references to ask for a reference. Ask for a hard copy of these references one week before the WSU deadline (<strong>December 1, 2014</strong>) to be provided to the director. (<a href="/prepare/">Tips for writing references</a>)</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="appprocess">Application Process</h2>
<ol>
	<li>Complete your application and turn in to the Director of Distinguished Scholarships by <strong>December 1, 2014</strong>. Include:
<ol>
	<li>14 questions of application + your answers to these questions.</li>
	<li>Three letters of reference mailed in a sealed envelope to the Director of Distinguished Scholarships, CUE 519 (postal code 4530).</li>
</ol>
</li>
	<li>Order transcripts from every college/university where you have received credit. These should be sent to:
<span style="padding-left: 12pt">Director, Distinguished Scholarships</span>
<span style="padding-left: 12pt">Smith Center (CUE) 519</span>
<span style="padding-left: 12pt">Washington State University</span>
<span style="padding-left: 12pt">Pullman, WA 99164-4530</span></li>
	<li>Reviews will be held with all applicants during the second week of school in January. Note:
<ol>
	<li>WSU may nominate only 4 applicants for the Truman Award.</li>
	<li>Nominees will receive online application details.</li>
	<li>WSU will write a letter of endorsement for each approved nominee.</li>
</ol>
</li>
	<li>Turn in your recent photograph.</li>
</ol>

<h3>After the Internal WSU Review</h3>
Chosen WSU nominees:
<ol>
	<li>Complete your essay drafts.</li>
	<li>Complete the online application. Carefully fill in all required fields!</li>
	<li>Confirm that your referees have completed the online review process.</li>
	<li>Proofread your work thoroughly.</li>
	<li>Submit your online application at least one week before the national deadline.</li>
</ol>

<h3>After you have submitted your application</h3>
<ol>
	<li>The Director of Distinguished Scholarships will review the application thoroughly.</li>
	<li>If there’s a problem, the director will contact you by phone to correct it.</li>
	<li>The director will complete the online process and submit each application.</li>
	<li>The director will mail required documentation to Washington, D. C. before national deadline.</li>
</ol>
