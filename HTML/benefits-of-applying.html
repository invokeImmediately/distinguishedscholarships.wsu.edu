Why apply for a distinguished scholarship?

Because you don't have to do it alone (and you shouldn't)!

The goal of applying, of course, is to win the award. However, the majority of applicants (award recipients or not) frequently talk about what they gain from the process.

By going through the application process students often:

  1. Engage in personal and professional assessment. Applying for a distinguished scholarship gives you a perfect opportunity to think about how you are developing as an individual and the ways your skills and experiences can be highlighted. It can also bring focus to your personal and professional goals.
  2. Identify gaps in their skills, knowledge, or experiences. Examining previous award winners' accomplishments will often help you spot gaps on your own resume. Finding the initiative to address skills gaps and pursue these opportunities puts you in a better position not only for a prestigious award, but also for your future career progression.
  3. Gain constructive feedback and increase their visibility. While it takes courage, asking for a review from faculty and other mentors often yields useful feedback about your academic efforts, research skills, and leadership capabilities. You are also encouraged to articulate these accomplishments in a brief yet compelling narrative./li>
