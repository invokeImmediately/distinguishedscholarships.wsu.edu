Workshops Offered by the Distinguished Scholarships Program

At WSU, undergraduate and graduate students partner with the Distinguished Scholarships Program to become competitive applicants for such life-changing, prestigious awards as the Rhodes, Marshall, Fulbright, Goldwater, and more. The following workshops are offered to help prospective applicants build their eligibility for a distinguished scholarship. All freshmen through graduate students and alumni are welcome at these events. Unless otherwise indicated, the duration of our workshops is one hour.


Introduction to Distinguished Scholarships

This interactive workshop will introduce you to several nationally-competitive scholarships available to WSU students. Many require that you be endorsed by WSU, so some workshops are mandatory—plus there are WSU deadlines to meet.

  • Thurs., Sept. 17, 2015 at 4:00 p.m. in Lighty 403

Your application for national scholarships must first be submitted to WSU for review on Dec. 1 for Boren, Gilman, Goldwater, Truman, and Udall awards; Feb. 29 for UK scholarships; and Aug. 22 for Fulbright awards.

The Benjamin A. Gilman International Scholarship

Gilman awards are for undergraduate study abroad and available to students who are receiving PellGrant funding; they support students who might not otherwise be able to participate in a study-abroad experience due to financial constraints.

Truman awards recognize college juniors with exceptional leadership potential who are committed to careers in government, the nonprofit or advocacy sectors, education, or elsewhere in public service. All students interested in applying this year must attend one of the Truman workshops.
