WSU’s newest Fulbright Fellow and Ph.D. student Patrick Freeze to improve Thailand rice by decontaminating soil

Tuesday, May 12, 2015—Washington State University doctoral student Patrick Freeze has received a Fulbright U.S. Student Program grant to spend 10 months in Thailand to study reducing toxic cadmium contamination and plant uptake in rural rice paddy soils, with a goal to improve the quality of the grain as a safe food source as well as an export product. Celebrate Distinguished Scholars 2015

Date: Wednesday, October 14, 2015 • Time: 3:30‑5:00 p.m. • Location: Distinguished Scholars Gallery (Ground level of CUB)

At our annual Celebrate Distinguished Scholars event, we honor recent and past WSU student recipients of prestigious, nationally competitive scholarships. The University's newest recipients of distinguished scholarships will be recognized and pinned. We also acknowledge the faculty and staff who have worked to bring success to the program and to each student pursuing a distinguished scholarship. Light refreshments will be served.


Distinguished Scholarships Program Reception

Date: Monday, April 20, 2015 • Time: 3:30‑4:30 p.m. • Location: Distinguished Scholars Gallery (Ground level of CUB)

At this invitation-only event, the Distinguished Scholarships Program celebrated all students who applied for nationally competitive, prestigious awards this year, the individuals who served as their references, and the members of the Distinguished Scholarships Program committees.


“International Scholarships”—Global Connections Webinar

Tuesday, April 7, 2015, 6pm—In this presentation, Sarah Ann Hones, director of WSU’s Distinguished Scholarships Program, discussed distinguished scholarships available to Global Campus students. She highlighted the Fulbright, Boren and Truman, talked about how scholarships could support family members, and offered tips on how to best impress the review committees with your application and resume.
