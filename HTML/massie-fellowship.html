More Details About the Fellowship

Applications are now open to undergraduate students for the Massie Family Endowed Fellowship at Yakima Area Arboretum (YAA). $2000 is available to award to a fellowship student for the 2016-17 academic year for the program at YAA.

  • Fellowship is awarded annually to an undergraduate student that is studying a major such as botany, landscape architecture, horticulture, wildlife ecology or environmental sciences, or others as appropriate to the mission and needs of the Yakima Area Arboretum.
  • Fellowship projects contribute directly to the long-range custodianship, oversight, and guardianship of YAA collections.
  • The fellowship mutually benefits YAA and WSU by providing an opportunity for a student to receive support to work on a real-world project under faculty direction that aligns with the evolving priorities, needs and projects of the Arboretum. The details of the fellowship project will be negotiated among the Fellow, the faculty mentor, and the YAA Executive Director or his/her designee.
  • The Fellowship recipient is expected to engage on site with the YAA and its staff in order to carry out the project. This means multiple trips to Yakima, and fellowship recipient will need to provide their own transportation. The final product of the fellowship will be shared with the relevant Arboretum staff in addition to the supervising faculty member.

Steps to Apply for the Fellowship

  1. Choose a project: review the list of current projects that can be completed at the Arboretum as part of the fellowship. You will write about what you would do with this project in the essay question entitled "Proposed Project" on the application.
  2. Apply online: complete the online application by November 15, 2016Exact details on deadline|i.e., the application will close at 12:01 a.m. on November 16, 2016.. After you submit your application, it will be automatically forwarded to Assistant Vice Provost for Undergraduate Education, Dr. Mary Sanchez-Lanier (sanchez@wsu.edu), for review.
  3. Evaluation by mentor: your supervising faculty member will evaluate your ability to carry out the proposal and communicate directly with Dr. Sanchez-Lanier.
  4. Awardee selected: the Vice Provost for Undergraduate Education, Assistant Vice Provost, and Undergraduate Education Scholarship Committee will select an awardee in consultation with YAA staff and donor. Applicants will be notified by December 15th.
