Massie Family Endowed Fellowship

Background on the Massie Family Endowed Fellowship

The Massie Family Endowed Fellowship is made possible by an endowment from DuwardBS '52 Agronomy with honors and the late Carolyn MassieFormer student, Nursing together with their children JenniferMS '79 Vocational Technical Education and Teaching Certificate, Jill, JeffreyBS '83 Civil Engineering, JessicaFormer student, Food Science, and JodyBA '90 Education and Teaching Certificate. Duward Massie is the first in his family to earn a college degree. His younger brother, Dale, followed Duward to WSU. In addition to his children who attended WSU, two sons-in-law also attended. Grandchildren of Duward and Carolyn Massie have attended WSU as well.

Duward Massie poses in front of YAA's Jewett Pond for a photo with Vice Provost for Undergraduate Education Dr. Mary Wack.

Duward worked as a plant scientist, using his professional education, talents, and abilities in multiple volunteer roles at the Yakima Area Arboretum (YAA) for over 40 years. The mission of the YAA is "to inspire people of all ages to discover and connect with nature through a diverse collection of trees and shrubs hardy to the Inland Northwest." In Duward's words, "This place is a community treasure." His goal in establishing this fund is to marry the two places he is passionate about, WSU and the YAA, in a relationship that will benefit both.
