Other Award Opportunities

Massie Family Endowed Fellowship

Available Projects

Applicants to the Massie Family Endowed Fellowship must select a project to complete at the Yakima Area Arboretum (YAA). These projects were designed by YAA staff to help address the current needs of the arboretum.

  1. Management Unit Report
    Project Description: Provide a full report on one management unit of the YAA collectionMore About YAA Collections|Click on this link to review a self-guided tour with more information on the collections of the YAA. (i.e., natural area, Jewett Pond, oaks, crabapples, or maples). The report should include a plant inventory, a study of the health of the area, future impacts of climate change, its history, any interesting facts, suggestions for any plants that should be added to the collection, and/or recommendations for its revitalization if needed.
  2. Apple Maggot Study
    Project Description: Study the effects of apple maggot on the YAA grounds. Research the role of apple maggot in the wider community and how it relates to the YAA. Look at how it is currently being managed on the YAA grounds, and assess its specific impact on the hawthorn and crabapple collection.
  3. Central Trail System
    Project Description: Design a new pathway through the YAA to highlight the collection, including side trails and make suggestions for pathway material and its total cost. Currently the YAA does not have a central trail system with connecting pathways.
  4. Children's Activity Book
    Project Description: Create a children’s activity book focusing on the educational components of the YAA, similar to the National Park Service’s Junior Ranger Program. The booklet would be geared toward kids 5-12 years old.
  5. Children's Play Garden
    Project Description: Design a children’s play garden and/or area on the YAA grounds for ages 3-12. The garden should encourage play and exploration in nature, and include features that will appeal to all five senses.
  6. Nature Day Camp Survey
    Project Description: Develop a parent/child survey to be used throughout the summer Nature Day Camp that focuses on desired camp themes before camp starts in June. Then spend at least a week observing and assisting with Nature Day Camp so you can learn more about it. At the end of the summer you will use the survey findings to develop curriculum for one week of camp for each age group. If approved, your curriculum will be used the following summer.
  7. Outdoor Amphitheater
    Project Description: Design an outdoor amphitheater that will be used as a venue for presentations, classes, etc.
